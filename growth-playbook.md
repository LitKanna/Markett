# YOLKO Growth Playbook

Learnings distilled from the customer-acquisition transcript, translated into
actions for a Flemington egg-tray business. Do the high-leverage moves first.

## Core principles → YOLKO moves

### 1. Give away something others charge for
Free value pulls leads faster than ads alone.

**YOLKO move:** After every booking, give the free **7 breakfasts from one tray**
guide (on the site). Use it in WhatsApp follow-ups and Facebook posts as the hook,
then route people to book.

### 2. Founder = marketing
Marketing skill does not survive a big committee. Fewer, better. You own hooks,
photos, and replies.

**YOLKO move:** You write every ad hook and take every product photo. Cap paid
help to delivery logistics only until weekly trays are consistently sold out.

### 3. Ask: “What would it take to be #1?”
Skip 10% thinking. Reverse-engineer the end state.

**YOLKO #1 definition:** The default place Homebush / Strathfield / Lidcombe /
Auburn households book Pace Farm trays for Flemington pickup.

**What else must be true:**
1. Standing WhatsApp list of weekly regulars (LTV)
2. Real tray photos + one clear offer everywhere
3. Partner nodes (cafés, Indian groceries, bakers) feeding you booked pickups
4. Sell-out scarcity that is true every week

Put nearly all weekly effort into those four, not random posts.

### 4. Sell in a vacuum (where or what)
Compete less by being the only option in the room, or selling something unique.

**YOLKO where:** Local Facebook groups + WhatsApp broadcasts (not supermarket aisle).
**YOLKO what:** Book-ahead reserved trays at a known Fri/Sat pickup — not “cheap eggs online.”

### 5. Pre > post
Idea and hook beat editing. An ounce of pre is worth a pound of post.

**YOLKO move:** Before recording a Reel or writing a group post, pick the hook from
the ranked list in `ads/facebook-instagram-ads.md`. Shoot once with the line locked.
Do not “fix it in CapCut.”

### 6. Minimize need-to-believes
Every extra belief kills conversion.

**One belief for YOLKO:** “I can book a fresh Pace Farm tray for $12 and pick it up
at Flemington Fri/Sat.”

Do **not** also sell: farm visits, delivery, organic vs cage debate, memberships.
Truth copy on the site answers doubts early so the form only asks for name + mobile.

### 7. Split-test ranking (permanent lifts)
Test in this order — one-time effort, ongoing gain:

1. **Offer** — tray / double / box / “first tray + free breakfast guide”
2. **Packaging** — headline + subhead
3. **Images** — real tray photo vs studio
4. **Checkout path** — Reserve+WhatsApp vs Stripe Buy now (third-party UX)

Track which Facebook primary text and which site CTA win for two weeks, then lock winners.

### 8. Ability to spend more = LTV (not cheaper leads)
Ads only get more expensive. Win by making each customer worth more.

**YOLKO levers:**
- Standing weekly order (checkbox on the form)
- Saturday-night “save you a tray next week?” message to every buyer
- Double-up default for families
- Raise price only after sell-outs are consistent (strongest LTV lever)

### 9. Affiliates that hurt (in a good way)
20% referral fees do not change behaviour. Peel a piece of the offer and let the
partner keep **100%** of that piece.

**YOLKO partner offer (café / grocery / baker):**
- Partner sells a **3-tray starter pack** for ≥ $36 and keeps every dollar
- Your hard cost ≈ wholesale of 3 trays
- You get a prepaid, qualified weekly buyer for upsells (box, standing order)
- Pitch: “Sell three trays to your customers, keep all the cash, we hold stock Fri/Sat”

Do this with 10 nearby food businesses before chasing influencers.

### 10. Truth is the asset
Damaging admissions raise belief in the good claims.

**YOLKO truths to say out loud:**
- Small operation, limited trays, we sell out
- Pickup only — no delivery
- Sealed Pace Farm trays we hold for people who book
- Not the fanciest stall — just a fair price and a reserved tray

Formula: **bad, bad, but good** — e.g. “Tiny stock. Pickup only. But your tray is
set aside before the market rush.”

## Weekly operating rhythm (from principles)

| When | Action | Principle |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Wed | Update stock, post 1 winning hook in 2–3 groups, WhatsApp regulars | Pre, #1 |
| Thu | One Reel from a pre-written hook; real tray photo | Pre > post |
| Fri / Sat | Fast replies; note who wants weekly | LTV |
| Sat night | “Want me to save your tray next week?” to every buyer | LTV |
| Monthly | Pitch 2 partner businesses the 3-tray keep-100% deal | Affiliates |
| Always | Founder writes hooks; split-test offer/headline/image | Talent, tests |

## What not to do
- Hire a “marketing person” before you can sell out on founder posts
- Chase cheaper Meta leads instead of weekly repeat buyers
- Add delivery, subscriptions software, or five SKUs before the one offer converts
- Soft-pedal scarcity if trays are actually limited — truth converts
